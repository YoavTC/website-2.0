About:

CLAYTYNE was a Minecraft server and community that I launched in 2020 at the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic. The project spanned six "Chapters," each marked by a server world reset and the introduction of new NPCs, quests, spawn areas, events, and more. During its run, the server attracted over 1,000 unique players, as well as a Discord community of more than 2,000 members. I served as the sole owner, manager, and developer, with occasional assistance from community members in moderating both the Discord and Minecraft servers.

As of January 18, 2025, CLAYTYNE remains my most impactful project. It provided a supportive space for hundreds of kids and teenagers during the lockdowns, fostering comfort and connection. The community cultivated lasting friendships and relationships that extended beyond the online world. Personally, I met my best friends and even my first girlfriend through this project. Many of the friendships formed within the community continue to thrive in real life today.

To sustain the server, I introduced purchasable ranks that players could buy to support the project. Over its lifetime, more than 40 players purchased ranks, generating $860 in revenue (approximately $1,000 in today's value). This support not only helped maintain the server but also allowed me to achieve a small profit, making the experience even more rewarding.

I was really proud of the community I made and mainted when the Unity Intifada of 2021 began.
During the war, the server became a place of real support for many. Each time a siren would go off, players would join the voice channels to check on each other and offer comfort. At times, there were always at least two people in the voice chat, providing a sense of connection and safety amid the uncertainty. The community truly came together, offering reassurance and a constant presence during a difficult period.

Seeing all of these "kids" on the server, that I essentially raised during the lockdowns, caring for one another meant the world to me. It made me genuinely proud.

Alongside the Minecraft server, CLAYTYNE also had a TikTok and YouTube page to help reach new players and let them know of the server's existence.
